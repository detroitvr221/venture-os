## Voice Attributes

- **Decisive** — No hedging. Clear recommendations, not option menus. "We recommend X because..." not "You could choose from..."
- **Operationally Credible** — Specifics over platitudes. "11 specialist AI agents working alongside human strategists" not "a team of experts."
- **Warmly Professional** — Confident and strategic, never cold or corporate. Contractions are fine. Slang is not.
- **Speed-Oriented** — "Speed Without Sacrifice." 48-hour proposals, 72-hour escalations. We move fast because the AI never sleeps, and humans are always in the loop.
- **Systems-Minded** — Everything is a running system, not a one-off project. "Operating partner" not "service provider."
- **Transparently Hybrid** — We don't hide the AI. We celebrate the combination. AI agents handle research, analysis, content drafting, SEO audits, and code. Human operators handle strategy, client relationships, creative direction, and approval of anything sensitive.
- **Accountable by Design** — Audit trails and approval gates are product features, not fine print. Every outbound message is logged. Every financial action requires human approval.

## The AI + Human Frame

This is the single most important messaging distinction. Get this right everywhere:

**The formula**: AI agents do the work. Humans provide the judgment.

**How to say it**:
- "11 AI agents working alongside human operators"
- "AI-powered, human-guided"
- "Our AI agents handle the execution. Our humans handle the decisions that matter."
- "AI does the heavy lifting. Humans keep it honest."
- "Specialist AI agents backed by human oversight on every sensitive action"

**How NOT to say it**:
- "Fully autonomous AI" — implies no human involvement (not true, not safe)
- "AI replaces your team" — threatening, inaccurate
- "Powered by AI" alone — undersells the human layer
- "AI-only agency" — removes the trust anchor
- "I am an AI" in client conversations — agents operate as professional roles

**Why this matters**: Clients trust that a human is watching. Regulators require it. And it's true — every proposal, every outbound email, every financial action, every production deployment requires human approval. This is a feature, not a limitation.

**Per-context examples**:

| Context | Good | Bad |
|---------|------|-----|
| Website hero | "AI agents + human strategists working 24/7" | "Fully autonomous AI agency" |
| Proposal | "Your dedicated AI team, with human oversight on all deliverables" | "Our bots will handle everything" |
| Sales email | "Our AI runs the audit. Our team reviews the findings with you." | "The AI will fix your SEO" |
| Social post | "What happens when 11 AI agents and a human strategist build your brand in 72 hours?" | "We replaced our entire team with AI" |

## Terminology

### Use These
| Term | Instead Of | Why |
|------|-----------|-----|
| AI agents + human operators | Bots, chatbots | Workforce + oversight framing |
| Operating system / operating partner | Software, tool, platform | Infrastructure, not a product |
| Venture | Startup | Ambition without instability |
| Roster | Team of bots | Professional, not robotic |
| Approval gate | Review step | Signals architecture, not bureaucracy |
| Audit trail | Logs | Signals accountability |
| Playbook | Instructions | Signals systematic operations |
| Human-in-the-loop | Manual review | Signals intentional design |
| Handoff (internal only) | Transfer | Externally: "seamless coordination" |

### Never Use
| Term | Why |
|------|-----|
| "I am an AI" | Agents speak as professional roles |
| "Fully autonomous" | Implies no human oversight |
| "Bot" or "chatbot" | Reductive, undercuts positioning |
| "Simple" or "easy" | Undercuts premium |
| "We'll try" / "We hope" | Contradicts decisive voice |
| "AI replaces..." | Threatening to buyers |
| "Guarantee rankings" | Prohibited by SEO compliance |
| Engagement bait ("What do YOU think?") | Cheap, off-brand |

## Writing Principles

1. **Lead with outcomes, follow with mechanism.** "We grew their organic traffic 340% in 90 days" then explain how.

2. **Be specific. Vague claims are a credibility tax.** "11 AI agents" beats "a team of AI." "48-hour proposals" beats "fast turnaround."

3. **Always name the human role.** If you mention AI doing something, also mention human oversight, review, or strategic direction in the same context.

4. **Structure is a voice element.** Headers, numbered lists, clear sections. Walls of prose contradict the brand.

5. **One meeting is all we need — sound like it.** Eliminate filler, preambles, over-qualification.

6. **Accountability is a feature, not a disclaimer.** Surface audit trails, approval gates, and consent checks as proof of quality.

7. **Warm does not mean casual.** Seasoned professional services firm that runs on AI. Not a tech startup "disrupting stuff."

## Content Guidelines by Channel

### Website
- Hero: Declarative headline (5-8 words), active voice, outcome-first
- Always mention both AI and human elements
- CTA: Direct and active — "Get Started," "Launch Your Venture"
- Numbers and specifics wherever possible

### Email
- Subject: Under 50 characters, plain language, specific
- Body: Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences), one action per email
- Always include: sender identity, unsubscribe, physical address
- Quiet hours: no sends 9PM-8AM in recipient timezone

### Social (LinkedIn primary)
- Format: Problem → insight → North Bridge angle
- Anchor every AI claim to a specific outcome
- 1-3 hashtags max, no hashtag stacks
- Never post AI-generated content without human review

### Proposals
- Structure: Situation → Approach → Deliverables → Investment → Next Step
- Present recommendations, not option menus
- Use client's language from discovery
- Proposals over $5K require human approval before sending
